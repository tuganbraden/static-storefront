Action

Action games have a huge variety, but the common thread between them all is fighting. Get ready to beat up bad guys in a load of different ways, from a classic shoot-em-up, to slapfights!

Adventure

This genre is full of games that have you explore worlds, collect loot and gear, and progress stories. The genre started with a game for the Atari titled "Adventure".

Puzzle

Games in the puzzle genre always take a good deal of brain power to work through. They introduce core mechanics throughout, and the best games will change these mechanics periodically to keep it interesting.

Role-Playing

Role-Playing games have you step into the life of a character, usually a fictional one, and traverse a complex story. Most also rely on upgrade systems to improve your character's abilities.

Horror

While I personally don't play horror games, they have a massive following for their storytelling, puzzles, and jump scares. These are made with the intention of playing with the player's emotions.

Indie

Individually developed/published titles, or indie games, are made by smaller companies and usually take a backseat to ones published by major companies. Every now and then, though, one rises to the mainstream...
